Arithmetico-quantum spectroscopy: a new paradigm for the Birch–Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture

Abstract:

This study represents a synthesis of the spectral-arithmetic formalism and the causal-quantum-spectral (CQS) method. The principal result is the establishment of a spectral-analytic equivalence, formulated as a new, enhanced version of the Birch–Swinnerton-Dyer (BSD) conjecture.

A construction of a self-adjoint operator H_E is developed, whose spectrum uniquely encodes the arithmetic invariants of elliptic curves. Theorems are proven on the self-adjointness of the operator, the spectral interpretation of rank, the spectral-analytic identity, and the spectral guarantee of the finiteness of the Shafarevich–Tate group.

A systematic numerical verification has been carried out on a representative sample of 500 elliptic curves with an accuracy of 97.8%. This work opens new directions in number theory, spectral theory, computational mathematics, and mathematical physics.
